# Institutional Collaboration Search Playbook

Use this when tracing partnerships between research institutes and universities, especially when the collaboration is not yet publicly announced.

## Techniques

### 1. Multi-pass keyword refinement

Start broad, then tighten based on gaps found in prior results:

- Pass 1: `InstituteName ResearcherName UniversityName Field Keywords`
- Pass 2: `ResearcherName ParentCompany Institute Collaboration Topic`
- Pass 3: `"ParentCompany" "University" "JointLab" Announcement Field`

### 2. Follow the institutional hierarchy

When searching for university-institute partnerships, search at the parent-company level too:

- A subsidiary institute may be under a larger parent organization
- Search both combinations: `${Subsidiary} ${University}` AND `${Parent} ${University}`
- The parent-level partnership may be publicly announced while the subsidiary-level collaboration is not

### 3. Cross-reference with professional societies

For researchers with ties to professional societies:

- Search society conference reports for institutional affiliations
- Annual conference programs often contain detailed presenter affiliations
- Example: a researcher may be listed under their new institution in a recent conference program, confirming a move not yet reflected in publications

### 4. When the target is "not yet public"

If a user heard about a collaboration from internal channels but there's no public record:

- State clearly: "No public record found"
- Present the closest public evidence
- Identify the structural gap that supports the claim
- Recommend the user confirm with the principals directly

## Example search patterns

| Target | Strategy |
|--------|----------|
| Institute-University partnership | Search both subsidiary and parent organization names with university |
| Researcher affiliation change | Search name + known institutions + professional society programs |
| New academic programs | Search `${Field} ${ProgramType} ${Year}` with multiple phrasing variants |

## Pitfalls

1. **Searching only at subsidiary level.** The parent organization's press releases may be the only public record.
2. **Assuming "no news = no collaboration".** Institutional partnerships often go public months after internal agreement.
3. **Missing professional societies as identity bridges.** Conference programs can surface affiliations not yet in publication records.
